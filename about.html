Things I do

I like science, technology, engineering, and math. In fact, some people (including me) refer to this as STEM so they can save 3 seconds of their life each time they mention it. Time is quite important in this world if I recall correctly. Here are a few of many things I do in my free time:

Short version:

  • Program robots (FRC team 1425)
  • Science research (ISEF '21, '22: I ♥ Nitrogen Detection)
  • Opened a startup organization (Start Tutoring)
  • Volunteer-tutor the underprivileged (& overprivileged) in STEM
  • Wrote & published a few childrens' detective fiction novels (secret pen-name)
  • Play the piano (Trinity College of London Grade 8 Pass) & the violin
  • Read books - mystery & sci fi & something about 42
  • Knitting, crochetting, embroidering, and general handicraftting (reuse the old!)
  • et cetera

Lengthy version:

  • ROBOTICS: I've participated in FIRST Lego League, FIRST Tech Challenge, and FIRST Robotics Competition from 5th through 12th grade. While my focus lies in the programming (java) aspect of the robots, I have recently grown into a more overall leadership position as the "robot integration lead," where I coordinate efforts of all the technical subteams over the process of designing, manufacturing, assembling, wiring, coding, and testing the robot. I love technology and all aspects of it.
  • RESEARCH: I started participating in science fairs in the 9th grade, and have been selected to join the Team Oregon delegation at ISEF every year since as either an Observer (9th grade), or as a Finalist (10th and 11th grade). The majority of my research has focused on developing a novel method to detect trace nitrogen concentrations in a sample. Contact me to learn more about my device & methodology!
  • TUTORING: I love helping people and teaching peers concepts that they didn't quite catch the first time around. I have tutored for over 40 hours every year so far (9th-11th grade), and received special recogniion from the Wilsonville HS Chapter of the National Honor Society for my outstanding volunteer hours. Then I decided to further organize tutoring efforts into a small organization, so I recently opened a startup ("Start Tutoring"). While my specialties lie in STEM fields, by opening this organization, I can hire specialized students in History and Language subjects who can tutor for those specific fields.
  • WRITING: I love to write, both fiction and nonfiction. I wrote & published a few childrens' detective fiction novels under the pen-name "Jana A. Bee." I also write for science in my school newspaper. I love to advocate for STEM! Writing is a fun passtime for me when I'm not reading. (My favorite novel series is "The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" by Douglas Adams, closely followed by anything by Agatha Christie.)
  • PIANO: I play the piano! I've been learning since I was 5 years old. I took a Solo Performance Exam by the Trinity College of London: Grade 8 PASS. I love music and my favorite composer is either Brahms, Chopin, or Tchaikovsky. Maybe Beethoven, or Mozart. I can't quite decide!!
  • VIOLIN: I play the violin, and started teaching myself to play in the 6th grade! 1st chair in my Middle School Orchestra (7th and 8th grade); selected to OMEA auditioned state orchestra (7th and 8th); Advanced High School Orchestra (9th).
  • CRAFTS: I love to craft, and figure out anything with patterns. Knitting, crochetting, embroidering, and general handicraftting (reuse the old!)
