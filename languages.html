Tamil (intermediate): I can speak it conversationally & read short sentences/signs & write basic words.
As my mothertongue, it was the first language I spoke, even before English. Although I havent taken many classes for it, I strive to teach myself by using only Tamil when spoken to in Tamil, and writing texts in Tamil to family members. My favorite feature is that the whole alphabet is arranged like a multiplication table, with the consonants against the vowels. They form a grid of (18+5)*13 letters, which are actually easy to recognise once the pattern is understood. The words are written as pronounced. In addition, there's also the case of "we" - there are different words for the "we" referring to 1) the person talking and the person being talked to and 2) the person talking and people besides the person being talked to. There are also many, many, many cultural phrases and metaphors used - all delightfully entertaining.
