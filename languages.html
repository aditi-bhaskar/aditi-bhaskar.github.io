Tamil (intermediate)
I can speak it conversationally & read short sentences/signs & write basic words.
As my mothertongue, it was the first language I spoke. I strive to teach myself by using only Tamil when spoken to in Tamil, or installing bilingual (or trilingual) keyboards on mobile. My favorite feature: the whole alphabet is arranged like a multiplication table, with the vowels against consonants. They form a grid of (18 + 5) * 13 unique letters. In addition, there's also the case of "we." 1) "we" refers to the person talking and the person being talked to and 2) "we" refers to the person talking and people besides the person being talked to. There are also many, many, many cultural phrases and metaphors used - all delightful.
